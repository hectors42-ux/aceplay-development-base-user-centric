01 · Position

The discipline of the ace.
The joy of play.

AcePlay sits at the intersection of tennis tradition and the modern player's reality. Centuries of ritual on one side. Gamified, mobile, social on the other.

The brand needs to feel serious about the sport — premium materials, editorial typography, restrained palette — and playful about the game — a single signature gesture, a kinetic visual device, a tagline that winks.

Not another tennis app. A consumer brand for amateurs and competitors who want their tennis to count.

02 · The Mark

One gesture. The serve.

A single ascending arc tracing the path of a perfect serve, terminating in the impact point. The arc is the mark, the system, and the brand's voice — drawn once and applied everywhere.

Primary · on cream
Reverse · on clay
On ink

What the arc means

The arc references the parabolic trajectory of a topspin serve — low at the racket, high at the apex, landing precisely on the T. It also reads as an ascending score, a level-up, progress.

The terminating dot is the ball's impact point — the ace.

Why a single gesture

A symbol with one move is recognizable at any scale, in any color, on any surface. It survives the favicon. It survives the billboard. It animates naturally — stroke-paint on splash, draws itself on first load.

The Cormorant "A" remains the secondary monogram for app icons and avatars where the arc would feel abstract.

03 · Wordmark

Cormorant Garamond. Ace upright, Play italic.

One typeface. Two registers. The tension between roman "Ace" (the discipline) and italic "Play" (the joy) is the entire brand in two syllables.

Primary wordmark · on cream
Reverse · on ink

Construction

Cormorant Garamond SemiBold 600 for "Ace", Italic Medium 500 for "Play". Letter-spacing tightened to -0.037em for display use. The "P" descender is allowed to breathe — never crop it.

Why italic Play

Italic is movement. It tilts the second syllable into action — fitting for the half of the brand that gamifies. The color flip (ink → clay) reinforces the shift. Read top-down: tradition becomes play.

04 · Lockup

Mark + wordmark + tagline.

Primary lockup · horizontal
Stacked variant

Construction rules

Mark height = cap height of the wordmark. Mark sits to the left of the wordmark with a gap equal to half the cap height. The mark's ball terminator aligns optically with the top of the "A".

Tagline sits below the wordmark, separated by a horizontal clay rule of 80px length. Tagline is set in DM Mono 11–14pt, all caps, 0.32em letter-spacing.

CLEAR SPACE · MINIMUM = 1/2 CAP HEIGHT ON ALL SIDES
